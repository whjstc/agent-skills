# Market Naming Scan

Use this reference before ideating names for a real category. The goal is not to copy competitors; it is to understand category language, avoid crowded patterns, and find credible whitespace.

## Search Plan

When browsing is available, use 6-12 targeted searches. Mix category, buyer, and job-to-be-done terms:

- `best [category] tools`
- `[category] SaaS`
- `[job to be done] software`
- `[buyer] [problem] tool`
- `[category] alternatives`
- `[category] Shopify app` or marketplace-specific query when relevant
- `[name candidate] software`
- `[name candidate] trademark`

For each relevant product, capture:

| Product | Domain | Category | Tagline | Naming Pattern | Takeaway |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

## Pattern Extraction

Group competitor names into naming lanes:

- Literal category names: `CookieX`, `PrivacyX`, `InvoiceX`
- Outcome names: `TrustX`, `RevenueX`, `SecureX`
- Detection/visibility names: `Radar`, `Lens`, `Trace`, `Probe`, `Monitor`, `Watch`
- Workflow/action names: `Flow`, `Pilot`, `Ops`, `Desk`, `Kit`
- Authority names: `HQ`, `Base`, `Stack`, `Cloud`, `Hub`
- Coined names: invented but pronounceable names
- Metaphor names: names that imply protection, clarity, navigation, or signal

## Whitespace Analysis

After grouping, identify:

- Crowded roots: words that appear often enough to feel generic
- Confusing neighbors: names that make the product sound like a different category
- Risk words: terms that imply legal, financial, security, medical, or compliance guarantees
- Underused credible angles: terms that fit the buyer and product but are not saturated
- Roadmap-safe terms: words broad enough for the next 2-3 likely product extensions

## Naming Direction Gate

After the scan, convert patterns into user-facing direction choices before generating the final list. The goal is taste calibration: users should choose the kind of name they want, not react to a random pile of candidates.

Offer 4-6 options tailored to the category:

| Option | Direction | Description | Good Fit | Tradeoff |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| A | Clear/descriptive | Category and job are obvious | Self-serve products, SEO, low-trust markets | Less distinctive |
| B | Technical/audit | Feels rigorous, diagnostic, evidence-based | Devtools, audit products, technical buyers | Can feel cold |
| C | Trust/privacy-led | Signals safety, accountability, governance | Compliance-adjacent products, business buyers | Crowded in privacy/security |
| D | Detection/visibility | Emphasizes seeing hidden behavior | Monitoring, analytics, runtime inspection | May sound like a feature |
| E | Coined/brandable | Ownable, compact, trademark-friendly | Long-term brand building | Needs explanation |
| F | Metaphorical | Memorable image or concept | Differentiated positioning | Needs strong tagline |

Ask one question:

> Which naming direction should I prioritize? Pick one or two. If you want speed, I can choose the best mix based on the market scan.

If the user delegates the decision, choose the best 2-3 directions based on:

- Buyer sophistication
- Market crowdedness
- Need for immediate comprehension
- Domain availability pressure
- Legal/claim-risk constraints

## Naming Guidance

Use market evidence to constrain candidate generation:

- If the category is crowded with literal names, include coined and metaphorical lanes.
- If the buyer needs immediate comprehension, keep at least half the shortlist descriptive or semi-descriptive.
- If the product is compliance-adjacent, avoid `proof`, `certified`, `legal`, `compliant`, `guarantee`, and similar certainty claims unless the product truly provides that.
- If the product may expand beyond the first feature, avoid names tied to one channel, format, or integration.
- If a competitor owns a distinctive coined style, do not create close variants.

## Output

Summarize the scan before recommending names:

1. Market map: 5-15 competitors or adjacent products
2. Naming patterns observed
3. Crowded words to avoid
4. Claim-risk words to avoid
5. Naming whitespace
6. How those findings shaped the shortlist

If the scan is based on memory or limited inputs instead of live browsing, state that clearly.
